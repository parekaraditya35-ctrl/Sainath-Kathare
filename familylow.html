What is Family Low?

We believe that every matrimonial dispute deserves a thoughtful and humane approach. Before resorting to litigation, we carefully assess every aspect of the relationship and strive to preserve family bonds through negotiation, mediation, and reconciliation wherever possible. When litigation becomes unavoidable, we represent our clients with dedication while protecting their legal rights and personal dignity.


Family law is the legal framework that regulates personal and domestic relationships. It governs matters such as marriage, divorce, child custody, adoption, maintenance, and inheritance. In India, these regulations are heavily influenced by a combination of general civil statutes and religion-based personal

Core Areas of Family Law

  • Marriage & Divorce:  Defines the legal requirements for a valid marriage and the grounds/processes for dissolving it.
  • Child Custody & Support: Determines guardianship, visitation rights, and financial support, primarily focusing on the child's welfare.
  • Adoption: Sets the legal procedures and conditions required to welcome a child into a new family structure
  • Maintenance: Ensures financial provision for spouses, children, or dependent parents during or after a separation.
  • Inheritance & Succession: Governs how property, assets, and wealth are distributed upon the death of a family member.

Primary Legal Frameworks in India

Because India is a diverse country, family law is unique in that it considers an individual's religious faith. The primary frameworks include:

  • Hindu Law: Governs Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs through codified acts, including the Hindu Marriage Act (1955) and the Hindu Succession Act (1956).
  • Muslim Law:  Largely uncodified, it is guided by Sharia principles and personal laws for marriage, divorce, and property.
  • Christian Law: Governed by statutes such as the Indian Divorce Act (1869) and the Indian Succession Act (1925)
  • Parsi Law: Governed by the Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act (1936).
  • Special Marriage Act (1954): A secular civil law that allows individuals of any religion or background to marry, providing a uniform legal code.

Understanding these categories can help you navigate how your specific rights and obligations are managed within your family unit.
