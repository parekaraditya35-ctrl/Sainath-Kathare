What is Criminal law?

Criminal law is the body of legal rules that defines conduct prohibited by the state because it threatens, harms, or endangers public safety and welfare. Unlike civil law, which resolves private disputes and focuses on victim compensation, criminal law is enforced by the government to punish offenders, deter future crimes, and rehabilitate those who violate the statutes.


The Core Elements of a Crime

For an individual to be held criminally liable, the prosecution must typically prove two fundamental elements:

  • Actus Reus: The physical element, meaning a voluntary illegal act or a legal omission to act when a duty exists.
  • Mens Rea: The mental element, referring to a guilty mind, criminal intent, recklessness, or knowledge of the wrongdoing.
