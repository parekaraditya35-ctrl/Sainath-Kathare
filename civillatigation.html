What is Civil Litigation?

Property disputes, injunctions, declarations, recovery proceedings, partition suits, and contractual disputes require careful legal strategy and in-depth analysis. Our systematic approach, detailed legal research, and strong courtroom advocacy enable us to effectively protect our clients' rights and interests in all civil matters.


Civil litigation is a legal process used to resolve disputes between individuals, businesses, or organizations where compensation or specific performance is sought instead of criminal punishment.

Common Types

  • Breach of Contract: Non-payment, failure to deliver goods, or violating agreement terms.
  • Property Disputes: Boundary conflicts, landlord/tenant issues, or real estate disagreements.
  • Torts/Personal Injury: Libel, slander, negligence, or malpractice.

The civil litigation process typically involves these structured phases:

  • Pre-Filing: Evaluating the facts, assessing the statute of limitations, and attempting negotiation or Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) to avoid court.
  • Pleadings: The plaintiff files an initial complaint detailing the claims. The defendant then responds with an answer, which can include counterclaims.
  • Discovery: Both sides exchange relevant documents, evidence, and witness information via depositions and interrogatories.
  • Trial & Judgment: If no settlement is reached, the case proceeds to court where a judge or jury hears the evidence and delivers a verdict.

Key Features

  • Based on preponderance of probabilities
  • Results in compensation or legal relief
  • Governed by Code of Civil Procedure (CPC), 1908

Example

If someone fails to pay money under a contract, the affected person can file a civil suit to recover the amount.
